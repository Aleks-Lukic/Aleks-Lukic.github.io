How it all started


I have always loved animals, but especially dogs. In early 2019 I was traveling around Serbia and helping out at Vučjak Shelter for a few days as I love being in the company of so many dogs and doing something good for them. After that I continued to support the Shelter financially from Germany, and during that time I met my wife Lima.

We always dreamed of a house and a dog together. It was clear to us that it should be a rescue dog, as we would rather give a poor street dog (of which so many already exist!) a good life than buy a specially bred pedigree dog. Lima then found Sava on Dejan's Instagram profile at the end of 2020 and knew immediately that this was "our dog". From now on the waiting started, because Corona made transport to Germany very difficult, and so in April 2021 after moving to our house we drove to Serbia ourselves to pick up Sava.

It was also immediately clear to us that we would drive as many dogs back as could fit in our car, so on the way back we were traveling with four dogs. Little Mila fell in love with our Sava and snuggled up to him. Since she didn't have a permanent home in Germany yet, we spontaneously took her in with us and looking back, we couldn't be happier about it. The two showed us how wonderful it is to give these poor creatures the chance of a new life in a real family. It's so nice to see how they sleep in the warmth on cuddly blankets - without hunger and fear.

We would love to take in all the dogs and regularly fantasize about how a third and fourth would fit into the pack. But since our families throw up their hands in shock at these thoughts and monthly donations alone are not enough for us, we would like to go one step further. We want to proactively build a safe place for dogs in Bosnia ourselves and enrich the lives of poor souls.





Project


Where I come from, most people are poor and don't care much about animal welfare because of their own concerns. Dogs are kept on short chains or confined to tiny kennels all their lives, where they serve as "living alarm systems." Those roaming free breed uncontrollably, pups rarely survive their first few days or are even carelessly discarded into rivers or landfills.

However, despite the lack of empathy and financial resources, the local government in Šekovići is very interested in working with a private animal shelter in the area. They are willing to support us with non-monetary goods such as building materials, firewood, and construction machinery. However, we also need another €9,000 to start building the buildings.





October 2022

We have bought a large piece of land near Šekovići in Bosnia and are now planning the next steps:

1. Construction of a fence to secure an area of ​​approx. 8,000 m²
2. Development of the area with electricity and water
3. Build a heated wooden shelter for at least 100 dogs (Bosnian winters can get bitterly cold!)

The land is located here: https://goo.gl/maps/uqZ5PsLTdmGmZFX88
